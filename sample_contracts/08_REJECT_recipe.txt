CLASSIC SPAGHETTI CARBONARA

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 20 minutes
Serves: 4

INGREDIENTS
- 400g spaghetti
- 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
- 4 large eggs (2 whole, 2 yolks)
- 100g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- 50g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- Freshly cracked black pepper
- Salt for pasta water
- 1 clove garlic (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS

Step 1 — Prepare the sauce base
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and egg yolks until well combined. Add the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmigiano Reggiano to the egg mixture. Season generously with freshly cracked black pepper. Whisk until the mixture forms a smooth, creamy paste. Set aside at room temperature.

Step 2 — Cook the pancetta
Place a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta (and garlic clove if using). Cook, stirring occasionally, for 8 to 10 minutes until the pancetta is crispy and golden, and the fat has rendered. Remove the garlic clove if used. Turn off the heat and leave the pancetta in the pan.

Step 3 — Cook the pasta
Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt the water generously — it should taste like the sea. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente, usually 8 to 9 minutes. Before draining, reserve at least 1 cup of pasta cooking water. Drain the pasta.

Step 4 — Combine everything
Add the hot drained pasta directly to the skillet with the pancetta. Toss well to coat the pasta in the rendered fat. Remove the skillet from any heat source — this is critical. Let the pasta cool for 30 seconds so it is hot but not boiling. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta while tossing vigorously. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time, tossing constantly, until the sauce becomes silky and coats every strand of pasta. The residual heat will cook the eggs without scrambling them.

Step 5 — Serve immediately
Plate the carbonara immediately. Top with additional grated Pecorino Romano and a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper. Serve hot.

TIPS AND NOTES
- Never add cream. Authentic carbonara gets its creaminess entirely from eggs, cheese, and starchy pasta water.
- Guanciale (cured pork cheek) is more traditional than pancetta and has a richer flavour.
- The key to avoiding scrambled eggs is to remove the pan from heat completely before adding the egg mixture.
- Work quickly — carbonara waits for no one.

STORAGE
Carbonara does not keep well. Eat immediately for best results. Leftovers may be refrigerated for up to 1 day and reheated gently with a splash of water, but the texture will not be as creamy.

WINE PAIRING
Pairs well with a crisp white wine such as Frascati or Pinot Grigio, or a light red such as Chianti Classico.
